About Me

For as long as I can remember, I’ve been fascinated by how things work — and more importantly, how they can be transformed into something extraordinary. I’m especially drawn to procedural generation and shader programming because they embody infinite possibility. A single algorithm can shape entire worlds; a few lines of code can bend light, color, and movement in ways that feel like magic. For me, making games isn’t just about creating mechanics or visuals — it’s about discovery. It’s about chasing the unknown, turning complexity into beauty, and crafting experiences that surprise, inspire, and linger with players long after they’ve put the controller down.
Currently in London, pursuing my Masters in Game Development at Kingston University.
