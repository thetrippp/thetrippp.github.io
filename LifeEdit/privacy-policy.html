Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 2026  ·  Applies to LifeEdit

Overview

LifeEdit is a personal day planning tool that runs entirely in your browser. This policy explains what data is accessed, how it is used, and what is stored.

Short version: LifeEdit does not collect, transmit, or store any of your personal data on external servers. Everything stays on your device and in your own Google account.

Data we access

When you sign in with Google, LifeEdit requests permission to access:

  • Your Google Calendar events — to create, update, and delete events you choose to sync from the app
  • Your basic Google profile — your name and profile picture, displayed in the app header

This access is granted through Google's OAuth 2.0 system and only becomes active after you explicitly click "Sign in with Google" and approve the consent screen.

How your data is used

Your Google Calendar data is used solely to:

  • Create calendar events when you sync a tile to Google Calendar
  • Update those events when you change a tile's time or label
  • Delete those events when you remove a synced tile

Your profile name and picture are displayed in the app header so you can confirm which account is signed in. They are not used for any other purpose.

Data storage

LifeEdit stores the following data locally in your browser's localStorage:

  • Your day planning tiles (labels, colours, times, dates)
  • Your Google OAuth access token and its expiry time
  • Your Google profile name, email, and picture URL

This data never leaves your device. It is not sent to any server operated by LifeEdit. Clearing your browser's site data will permanently remove it.

Third-party services

LifeEdit integrates with the following Google services:

  • Google Identity Services — for authentication and OAuth token management
  • Google Calendar API — for syncing tiles to your calendar
  • Google OAuth2 UserInfo API — for retrieving your name and profile picture

These services are governed by Google's Privacy Policy. LifeEdit does not share your data with any other third parties.

Revoking access

You can revoke LifeEdit's access to your Google account at any time by visiting myaccount.google.com/permissions and removing LifeEdit from the list of connected apps. You can also click "Sign out" within the app, which revokes the token immediately.

Children's privacy

LifeEdit is not directed at children under the age of 13 and does not knowingly collect any information from children.

Changes to this policy

If this policy is updated, the "Last updated" date at the top of this page will change. Continued use of the app after changes constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
